Magnificent Temples

Explore the two greatest temple complexes of the Khmer Empire - Angkor Wat and Angkor Thom, each telling a unique story of architectural mastery and spiritual devotion

Must visit

ប្រាសាទ អង្គរវត្ត

Angkor Wat is the largest religious temple in the world, located in Siem Reap, Cambodia. Built in the 12th century by King Suryavarman II, it was originally a Hindu temple dedicated to Vishnu, later becoming a Buddhist site. Its architecture reflects Khmer ingenuity and spiritual devotion.

Must visit

ប្រាសាទ បាយ័ន្ដ

Bayon Temple, located in Angkor Thom, Cambodia, is famous for its serene stone faces carved on towering towers. Built in the late 12th century by King Jayavarman VII, it represents the center of the Khmer Empire. Its unique architecture and spiritual symbolism make it a masterpiece of Khmer art.

Must visit

ប្រាសាទ បន្ទាយស្រី

Banteay Srei is a 10th-century Cambodian temple dedicated to the Hindu god Shiva. Known for its pink sandstone and intricate carvings, it stands out for its fine detail and small scale. Located near Angkor, it is often called the "Jewel of Khmer Art" for its exceptional beauty.

Must visit

ប្រាសាទ​ តាព្រហ្ម

Ta Prohm is a famous Khmer temple in Siem Reap, Cambodia, known for its ancient trees growing through the ruins. Built in the late 12th century by King Jayavarman VII, it was a Buddhist monastery. The temple's unique blend of nature and stone makes it a popular tourist attraction.

Must visit

ប្រាសាទ​ ព្រះខ័ន្ធ

Preah Khan is a majestic temple in Siem Reap, built in the 12th century by King Jayavarman VII. It served as a religious and administrative center. Surrounded by jungle, it features intricate carvings, long corridors, and symbolic Hindu-Buddhist elements. Preah Khan reflects ancient Khmer architectural brilliance.

Must visit

ប្រាសាទ​ បាពួន

Ba Phun Temple is an ancient Khmer temple located in Cambodia. Known for its beautiful sandstone carvings, it reflects the rich cultural and religious heritage of the Khmer Empire. The temple features traditional architecture and serves as a historical site attracting visitors interested in Cambodia’s history and spirituality.

Must visit

ប្រាសាទ ភ្នំបាខែង

Phnom BaKheng Temple is an ancient Khmer temple located near Siem Reap, Cambodia. Built during the late 9th to early 10th century under King Yasovarman I, it features sandstone structures and intricate carvings. Though smaller than Angkor Wat, it showcases early Khmer architecture and religious significance as a Hindu worship site.

Must visit

ប្រាសាទ​ នាគព័ន្ធ

Neak Pean is a small, beautiful temple located on an artificial island in the middle of a large reservoir near Angkor, Cambodia. Built in the 12th century by King Jayavarman VII, it was dedicated to healing and spiritual balance. Its unique design features connected pools symbolizing elements of health and well-being.

Must visit

ប្រាសាទ តាសោម

Ta Som Temple is a small Hindu-Buddhist temple located in the Angkor Archaeological Park, Cambodia. Built in the late 12th century by King Jayavarman VII, it features beautiful stone carvings and a famous “face tower.” The temple is known for its peaceful atmosphere and intricate architecture.

Must visit

ប្រាសាទ បក្សីចាំក្រុង

Baksi Chamkrong is a small Hindu temple located in the Angkor Archaeological Park, Cambodia. Built in the early 10th century by King Harshavarman I, it is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is known for its intricate sandstone carvings and well-preserved architecture, representing the Khmer Empire's early classical style.

Must visit

ប្រាសាទ​ ចៅសាយទេវតា

Chau Say Tevoda is a beautiful Hindu temple located near Angkor Thom in Cambodia. Built in the 12th century during the reign of King Suryavarman II, it features intricate carvings of Hindu gods and goddesses. The temple is dedicated to Shiva and Vishnu and showcases classic Khmer architecture.

Must visit

ប្រាសាទ​ ក្រវ៉ាន់

Krovan Temple, located near Angkor Thom in Cambodia, is a small but beautifully preserved Khmer temple. Built in the 10th century during the reign of King Rajendravarman II, it is known for its three brick towers dedicated to Hindu gods. The temple’s intricate carvings and red brick structures showcase classic Khmer architecture.
